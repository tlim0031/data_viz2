Tourism in Malaysia

This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of Malaysia's tourism landscape.
It features demographic insights into tourist arrivals, purpose of their
visits, popular shopping locations among tourists and also understanding
tourist's expenditure patterns. These data helps the stakeholders such as
travel agencies and hospitality provider to refine on marketing strategies.
Tourism board can also help create better travel experiences for
international tourist and enhance the tourism sector.

Overview of International Tourist Arrivals at Different States of Malaysia in 2023

The sudden decline of international arrivals in April 2020
has shown the impact of Covid-19 pandemic on Malaysia's
tourism industry. Major lockdowns and travel bans explains
why the number of arrivals approaches 20,000 since April 2020
despite having a total arrival of 2.9 million in January.
In 2021, the spike in tourist arrivals in December demonstrates
post-covid recovery and tourist start to travel. In 2022,
there is a steady increase in international arrivals throughout
the year and reaches a peak of 2.4 million in December.
In 2023 and 2024, the tourism industry starts to stabilize,
as shown by the even distribution of monthly tourist arrivals.
The balanced gender proporition in tourist arrivals also shows that
both male and female are equally engaged in traveling.

Top Searches by Tourists

Q1: January to March, Q2: April to June,
Q3: July to September, Q4: October to December

The number of searches for "Published Packages" is consistently
high across all four quarters. In contrast, "Agents" has the
least number of searches among the four categories, indicating
tourist's preference on self-planned trip rather than relying on
travel agents to handle their trip arrangements. Tourists also
seemed to be particularly interested in "Published Deals" but
the frequency of searches fluctuates more across quarters
compared to "Published Packages". It appears that the tourist's
search interest on various travel deals is driven by specific
travel periods, such as during peak travel seasons in quarter
3 and quarter 4. Additionally, search patterns for "Merchant"
appear to be quite uniform, highlighting its significant role
in travel planning.

Purpose of Visit

"Holiday" appears to be the most prominent reason for visit,
indicating that majority of people travel for leisure rather than
for business, education or other purposes. Furthermore, a lot of
international students choose to pursue education in Malaysia for
better educational opportunities, affordable tuition fees and
unique courses. The diverse and multicultural environment also
make Malaysia a preferred choice for exchange programs because
students get to immerse themselves in the local culture.
In contrast, business travel appears to be lesser mainly due
to the global shift towards hybrid working mode where most
as most companies will conduct their business operation online,
which reduces the need for frequent business travel.

Tourist Spending Patterns

* Click on the legend to filter the categories
and click on white space to remove filter.

Accommodation and tourism characteristic products appear to be
the top spending categories for tourists. High proportion of expenses
in tourism characteristic products category highlighted significant
importance of local cultural experience. Transport and food and
beverage expenses are on par, indicating a balance in travel
logistics and culinary enjoyment. Both entertainment and travel
agencies occupies a relatively low proportion among the expenses
suggest that tourist prefer planning their own itinerary rather
than standardized packaged offered by travel agencies. Hence, they
would conduct more research to plan their trips efficiently.
